Dave Bickler Financial Overview

Early Career and Initial Earnings

Dave Bickler started his music career in 1968 with Jamestown Massacre. Their single Summer Sun set the stage for his future success. Although his initial earnings were modest, they were essential in launching his career.

Success with Survivor and Major Income Sources

Bickler joined Survivor in 1978. The band’s big break came with Eye of the Tiger in Rocky III. The song won a Grammy and brought substantial financial success. Eye of the Tiger royalties have been a significant part of his income.

Other hits like American Heartbeat and Caught in the Game also contributed to his earnings.

Financial Impact of Medical Issues and Band Departures

In 1983, Bickler faced vocal cord surgery, affecting his earnings due to a performance hiatus. He returned to Survivor in 1993, but legal issues within the band limited new releases and income.

Post-Survivor Career and Additional Income Streams

After leaving Survivor in 2000, Bickler focused on commercial jingles. His work on the Budweiser Real Men of Genius campaign was very successful, providing a steady income. He recorded over 100 commercials, which significantly contributed to his financial stability.

Solo Career and Recent Projects

In 2018, Bickler released his debut solo album, Darklight. This project marked a new chapter in his career, allowing him to showcase his talent as a solo artist.

Performances and appearances in recent years, including at festivals and on television shows, have also contributed to his income. Despite the challenges, Bickler’s resilience and talent have ensured a steady flow of earnings from various sources.
